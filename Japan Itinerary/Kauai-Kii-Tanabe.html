Arrivals
HND Terminal 3 — Immigration, Baggage & Customs Flight arrives 7:25 PM JST  •  Allow ~45–60 min through to Arrivals Lobby
✓ Visit Japan Web registered (vjw-lp.digital.go.jp) — Both travelers registered under one account. QR codes are already generated and waiting in your VJW account — no action needed at the airport beyond opening the site.

VJW is a web app, not an App Store download. Access it via Safari at vjw-lp.digital.go.jp. Any “Visit Japan Web” apps in the App Store are unofficial third-party apps — ignore them. To make it feel native: in Safari tap Share → Add to Home Screen — this installs VJW as a PWA icon on your iPhone. Do this before departure.

To reach your QR code: Home screen → Register planned entry/return → tap “Kumano Kodo” → select the Applicant (Matthew or Mary) → tap “Display QR Code” at the bottom → QR displays on next screen. Each traveler has their own QR; it covers both immigration and customs in one code — repeat by going back and selecting the other Applicant.
Account (digital.go.jp): mattwongucb@gmail.com — password shared with Mary via secure password manager.

⚠ Screenshots and printed QR codes are NOT accepted — must be displayed live from the VJW site. Before boarding at HNL: log in to VJW on Wi-Fi and activate offline mode (tap “Use the Visit Japan Web site offline” in the menu); QR codes will then display without internet through immigration. Keep the browser open — do not close Safari. VJW replaces all paper landing cards; no forms to fill out on the plane.
  1. 1
    Disembark → Follow Arrival Signs (入国審査) After exiting the aircraft follow overhead signs for Arrivals / Immigration. Foreign nationals proceed to the Foreign Passport lanes — separate from Japanese national / residence card holders.
  2. 2
    Joint Kiosk — Combined Immigration & Customs QR (one per person) HND uses Joint Kiosks that handle immigration and customs in a single scan — there is no separate customs stop later. Each traveler goes through their own kiosk. Open VJW in Safari → tap “Kumano Kodo” → select the Applicant (Matthew or Mary) → tap “Display QR Code”. Scan the QR, place fingers on the fingerprint reader, look into the camera — gate opens on match. Go back, switch Applicant, and repeat for the second traveler. No passport stamp is issued at the kiosk. If a kiosk is closed or errors, proceed to a staffed counter; the officer will verify VJW on screen or issue a paper card.
  3. 3
    Baggage Reclaim (手荷物受取) Proceed to the Baggage Claim hall. Check the overhead monitors for your belt number (flight HA831 / AS831 from HNL). Collect both checked bags. If a bag has not appeared within 30 minutes, report to the Baggage Services desk before exiting the secure area — you cannot re-enter once through customs.
  4. 4
    Green Channel (税関) — No Scan Needed Customs was already declared at the Joint Kiosk in Step 2 — no second scan required. After collecting bags, follow signs for 税関 / Customs and take the green channel (nothing to declare). An officer may wave you through or briefly inspect bags; this is routine. Duty-free allowances per person: alcohol 3 bottles (760 ml ea) • tobacco 200 cigarettes • gifts & goods up to ¥200,000.
  5. 5
    Exit to Arrivals Lobby (2F, T3) After customs you are in the public Arrivals Lobby. Seven Bank ATM is on 2F here — withdraw JPY now (see hotel card below). Hotel Villa Fontaine Grand Haneda is connected directly to T3 via a covered walkway; follow hotel signs from the Arrivals Lobby.

⚠ Buy Yamabiru-fai (Leech Spray) Ask the staff for it here — it is highly recommended to have this before reaching Takijiri. Apply to shoes, socks, and lower legs before starting the trail. The cedar forest is prime leech habitat, especially after rain.

Pick up the free Pilgrims’ Passport booklet. Stamps are collected at designated checkpoints along the Nakahechi route — this is your official record for the Dual Pilgrim Certificate (Kumano Kodo + Camino de Santiago).
