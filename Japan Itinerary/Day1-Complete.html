  1. 1
    Takijiri-oji  滝尻王子  ■ Stamp
    Visit Takijiri-oji Shrine — the traditional starting point of the Kumano Kodo Nakahechi Route. Register at the Takijiri Pilgrimage Center (Kumano Kodo Kan) inside the building at the trailhead. Collect your second stamp here (your first was at Tokei-jinja this morning). Take time to explore the shrine grounds and the sacred atmosphere of this UNESCO World Heritage pilgrimage starting point.
    🚽 Restroom available here — Inside the Kumano Kodo Kan visitor center.
    Wikimedia Commons — KMR, CC BY-SA 3.0
  2. 2
    Kumano Kodo Kan  熊野古道館
    The visitor center and museum at the Takijiri trailhead. Exhibits cover the history and spiritual significance of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage in English and Japanese — route maps, artifacts, and information about the sacred grand shrines ahead. Allow 30–60 minutes to explore at a relaxed pace.
    🏭 Practical notes: Free entry. Staff can answer questions about the trail and local conditions. Wi-Fi may be available.
  3. 3
    Free Time  自由時間 — Takijiri Village
    Takijiri sits where the Hiki River runs alongside the road — a peaceful spot to rest and take in the mountain surroundings. Vending machines and a small rest area are near the trailhead. A good time to eat lunch, review tomorrow’s trail plans, or simply sit and enjoy the forested setting.
    ☕ Teahouse at Takijiri-oji — A small chaya (茶屋) near the shrine serves hot tea and local sweets. A perfect rest stop before your walk to the guesthouse — sit, enjoy the mountain air, and take in the trailhead atmosphere.
    🌳 For tomorrow’s hike: Pick up walking sticks and a bear bell at the Kumano Kodo Kan while you’re here — both are available at the center and strongly recommended for the trail ahead.
  4. 4
    Guest House Kiyohime  清姫 — Check-In
    Road walk from Takijiri-oji to Guest House Kiyohime — 2.0 km • 28 min • Mostly flat along Route 311. Confirm check-in time with the owner via MyChat / WhatsApp.
