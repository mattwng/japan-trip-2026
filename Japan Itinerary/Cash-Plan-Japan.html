Cash Plan — Japan

Matthew Wong • Mary Siu • May 2026 • Kumano Travel Res #186433
Revolut (ATM) • Chase VISA + Apple Pay • Mobile Suica (iPhone) • Two ATM Stops
Overview

This plan covers the full payment stack for Japan travel: ATM cash via Revolut, card payments via Chase VISA, transit via Suica, and a strategic cash withdrawal schedule tied to the itinerary. Hotels, trains, taxis, and card-accepting restaurants go on Chase VISA. Cash is reserved only for fixed obligations — rural buses, onsen taxes, Takkyubin, stamps, and small cash-only vendors.

💳 Revolut → ATM Cash 💳 Chase VISA → Apple Pay 🚃 Suica → iPhone Wallet
1 · Revolut Card — Primary ATM Source
Revolut CardFund in advance • Withdraw JPY from ATMs in Japan
ATM
Before Departure
  • Load $200 USD on each card ($400 combined, ~¥60,000 available)
  • Enable JPY currency in the Revolut app (Accounts → Add currency)
  • Confirm ATM withdrawal limits (standard: $1,200/month fee-free, then ~2% fee)
  • Enable for international use (Security → Location-based security: off, or add Japan)
  • Note your PIN — ATMs in Japan require PIN for all withdrawals

Tips
  • Withdraw in larger amounts to minimize per-transaction fees

  • 7-Eleven ATMs are open 24/7 and widely available everywhere
2 · Chase VISA — Card Payments & Apple Pay
Chase VISAPrimary card • Apple Pay for contactless where supported

Apple Pay
  • Add Chase VISA to Apple Wallet (if not already added)

Accepted at convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart), department stores, hotels, larger restaurants, and electronics stores.
Where Chase VISA Is Most Useful
  • Department stores (Isetan, Takashimaya, etc.)
  • Hotels — all accommodations on this trip accept card
  • Larger restaurants and chain restaurants
  • Electronics stores (Yodobashi, Bic Camera)
  • Tourist attractions with card readers
⚠️ Credit cards are NOT accepted at many smaller restaurants, izakayas, local shops, vending machines, and most shrines/temples — always carry cash.
3 · Suica — Transit & Convenience Payments
Mobile Suica (iPhone)Rechargeable IC card • Trains, buses & convenience stores
SUICA
Setup
  • Open Apple Wallet → tap + → search "Suica" → Create new Suica card
  • Works on: Tokyo Metro, JR lines, Shinkansen (IC), most city buses
Funding the Suica App
Option A — Chase VISA via Apple Pay  RECOMMENDED In Apple Wallet, tap your Suica card → Add Money → select Chase VISA from Apple Pay. Most reliable option; Visa cards generally work for Suica top-ups.
Option B — Revolut Card via Apple Pay  TRY FIRST Add Revolut to Apple Wallet, then use the same Add Money flow. Many Revolut Visa cards work for Suica top-ups — worth trying as your first backup. If rejected, switch to Chase VISA or the station kiosk.
Option C — Cash at Station Kiosk  BULLETPROOF Any JR station has green "Suica" kiosks that accept cash for top-ups. Use this if card top-up fails.
Load ¥3,000–¥5,000 on arrival. Top up at any station kiosk or via Apple Pay as needed.
Where Suica Works Beyond Transit
  • 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart, NewDays (convenience stores)
  • Vending machines (especially in train stations)
  • Some fast food chains (McDonald’s, Yoshinoya)
  • Coin lockers in stations
