Palace
Changdeokgung Palace — Main Grounds Jongno-gu  •  9:30 AM  •  Enter via Donhwamun gate

Admission: FREE for visitors aged 65 and older (all nationalities including foreign tourists) — show passport at the main gate ticket booth.

Injeongjeon (Throne Hall): Ceremonial heart of the palace, with Western-influenced stained-glass windows added during the Japanese colonial era — a jarring but historically telling detail.

Huijeongdang & Daejojeon: The royal living quarters, furnished as they appeared in the late Joseon period. More intimate scale than Gyeongbokgung.

AdmissionFree (65+ senior rate)
Duration~60–75 min; be at Huwon entrance by 11:15 AM

Buyongji Pond: A square pond (earth) with a round island (heaven), ringed by pavilions including the elegant Buyongjeong. One of the most photographed scenes in all of Seoul.

Yeongyeongdang: A rare commoner-style residence built inside the palace — deliberately plain by royal decree.

Ongnyucheon Stream: A winding granite channel carved for the royal poetry ceremony, where cups of wine were floated downstream while scholars composed verses.

Afternoon — Ikseon-dong & Rest
Lunch
Ikseon-dong Hanok Village Jongno-gu  •  1:00 PM  •  Walk ~8–10 min east from Changdeokgung

A tightly woven grid of restored hanok converted into cafes, wine bars, restaurants, and independent boutiques. One of the few places in central Seoul where the alleyways still feel genuinely old.

Look for gukbap (rice-and-soup), dumplings, or Korean fusion spots. Wander the surrounding alleys after lunch — vintage shops, pottery studios, and concept cafes tucked between the hanok walls.

Est. Cost (2p)~₩30,000–₩50,000
PaymentCash preferred at smaller spots; card at larger venues
