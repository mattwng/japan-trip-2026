Morning — Mangwon Market & Mangridan-gil
Transfer
Hotel Cappuccino → Mangwon Market (망원시장) Gangnam → Mapo-gu  •  Depart 10:30 AM

Taxi (Kakao T): ~20–25 min, ~₩14,000–₩18,000. Direct run across the Han River into Mangwon-dong — ask the driver for “Mangwon Market” (망원시장).

Cost (2p, taxi)~₩28,000–₩36,000
Market
Mangwon Market Exploration Mangwon-dong, Mapo-gu  •  11:00 AM

One of Seoul’s most authentic neighbourhood markets — a covered indoor+outdoor maze of food stalls, produce, and old-school Korean snacks. Far less touristy than Gwangjang.

Must-try street snacks:

  • Ssiat Hotteok — sweet seed-filled pancake; look for the stall with the longest queue
  • Gochu-twigim — deep-fried large green peppers stuffed with pork; crispy and addictive
  • Dakgangjeong (Q’s) — sweet & spicy Korean fried chicken bites
  • Tteokgalbi — grilled short-rib rice cakes; sweet and smoky
Est. Cost (2p)~₩10,000–₩20,000
PaymentCash only at all stalls
Walk
Mangridan-gil Boutique Walk Mangwon-dong, Mapo-gu  •  12:30 PM  •  ~15–20 min toward Han River

Exit the market and wander down Mangridan-gil — a quiet residential street lined with specialty cafés, concept stores, and independent boutiques far from the tourist trail.

Look out for:

  • Crow Canyon Showroom — beautifully crafted Korean enamelware and kitchen goods; ideal for a small, easy-to-pack souvenir
  • Deep Blue Lake Coffee — Nordic-style specialty coffee in a minimal concrete space
Est. Cost (2p)~₩5,000–₩15,000 (coffee & browsing)
Afternoon — Han River & Battleship Park
Riverside
Mangwon Hangang Park — Riverside Downtime Mangwon Hangang Park  •  1:00 PM  •  Mangridan-gil empties directly onto the park

Intentional, unstructured time by the water. One of Seoul’s quieter riverside parks; far less crowded than Yeouido, with long grassy banks and a gentle bike path.

Options:

  • Sit on the grass or a riverside bench and watch the river
  • GS25 or CU riverside convenience store — grab a cold drink or snack
  • Starbucks Mangwon Hangang (floating pier) — proper seat over the water

Seoul Battleship Park is a ~5 min walk — depart at 3:20 PM to arrive at 3:30 PM.

Est. Cost (2p)~₩5,000–₩10,000 (drinks & snacks)
Museum
Seoul Battleship Park (서울함공원) Mangwon Hangang Park  •  3:30 PM  •  ~30–45 min

A decommissioned Korean naval destroyer and support vessel, permanently moored at the Han River as an open-air museum. Walk the decks, explore the below-deck exhibits, and take in river views from the bridge.

Admission (2p)~₩3,000 at senior rate (₩1,500/person, 50% off)
Sunset
Sunset at Han River — Mangwon Hangang Park 5:00 PM  •  Starbucks Mangwon Hangang floating pier
🎁 Han River Convenience Store Tradition Stop at the riverside GS25 or CU: buy a paper bowl and instant ramen packet, use the hot water machine on the spot. The classic low-cost, high-vibe Seoul experience at the river.

Settle in as the afternoon light shifts over the Han. The floating Starbucks pier extends into the river — 360° river views over a drink.

Depart for dinner at 6:15 PM — short taxi (~8–10 min, ~₩5,000–₩8,000) to Baeknyeon Tojong Samgyetang in Hongdae.

Est. Cost (2p)~₩5,000–₩10,000 (coffee & snacks)
Evening — Dinner in Hongdae
Dinner
Baeknyeon Tojong Samgyetang (백년토종삼계탕) 118 Yanghwa-ro, Mapo-gu  •  6:30 PM  •  Near Hongik University Station Exit 3

One of Seoul’s most celebrated samgyetang institutions, using native Korean tojong chickens (indigenous breed, richer flavour than commercial). Short taxi from Mangwon Hangang Park into Hongdae — entirely within the same Mapo-gu pocket of the day.

삼계탕 Samgyetang: A whole young chicken stuffed with glutinous rice, ginseng root, jujube, and garlic, simmered into a milky-white, deeply restorative broth. Each person receives their own pot. Season to taste with the salt and black pepper at the table.

Return to hotel: Taxi from Hongdae to Hotel Cappuccino (~25–30 min, ~₩18,000–₩22,000 via Kakao T).
