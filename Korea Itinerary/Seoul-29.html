Guided Tour

Primary palace of the Joseon Dynasty, followed by a guided walk through Bukchon Hanok Village — a hillside residential neighbourhood of restored traditional hanok houses with sweeping rooftop views across central Seoul. Royal Guard Ceremony at the Gyeongbokgung main gate — great photo opportunity.

Latecomers and no-shows cannot be refunded. Full refund if cancelled at least 24 hours before activity (Seoul local time). Present mobile or printed voucher on arrival.

Midday — Gwangjang Market & Insadong
Lunch
Gwangjang Market Jongno-gu  •  ~12:45 PM  •  Tour ends here

Korea’s first permanent market — a classic Seoul food experience.

Must try: Bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes, freshly griddle-fried) • Mayak Gimbap (small, addictive rice rolls — “narcotic gimbap”). Browse the indoor stalls — most vendors cook to order; portions are individual-sized, ideal for sampling across multiple stalls.

Est. Cost (2p)~₩30,000–₩40,000
Per person~₩15,000–₩20,000
PaymentCash only at food stalls
Transfer
Gwangjang Market → Anguk Station Jongno-gu  •  Walk ~20 min through Ikseon-dong

Head north from Gwangjang Market through Ikseon-dong — a charming alley district of converted hanok cafés, wine bars, and independent boutiques. A natural transition from the market bustle into the quieter, older Seoul of Anguk. Follow Ikseon-ro north all the way up to Anguk Station.

Explore
Insadong Afternoon Walk Insadong, Jongno-gu  •  Start from Anguk Station (Line 3)

Enter Insadong from the south and walk north along Insadong-gil, browsing galleries, craft shops, antique stores, and tea houses.

Ssamziegil: Open-air spiral courtyard mall for independent shops, street food, and souvenirs.

Side alleys (Insadong-gil 8-gil, 12-gil, etc.): Hidden cafés, art galleries, and gems like Joseon Salon, KOTTON.SEOUL, and Saegimsori (handmade stamp shop — have a custom stamp carved on the spot). Stone stamp: square seal, red ink, three characters — 柴柔術. Est. ~₩30,000–₩50,000 per stamp. Cash only.

Graze as you go: Yesul Gimbap or Doma for a late lunch • Kotton Coffee mid-afternoon • Gabae Langsom for bingsu.

Est. Cost (2p)~₩60,000–₩100,000 (incl. stamp & grazing)
PaymentCash preferred at stalls; card at larger venues

Evening — Rest & Dinner
Rest
Rest Break (4:00 PM) Insadong  •  30–45 min off your feet before dinner

Joseon Salon (Insadong-gil) — modern Korean tea and cocktail bar with comfortable seating and a calm atmosphere; ideal for a pre-dinner aperitif. Alternatively, Gabae Langsom serves bingsu in a quiet courtyard.

Depart the café at 4:30 PM for the ~20 min walk south through Tapgol Park to Jogabi — arrive at 5:00 PM.

Est. Cost (2p)~₩10,000–₩20,000
Dinner
Jogabi Seafood Bar (조가비) — Early Dinner 21 Myeongdong 9-gil, Jung-gu  •  5:00 PM  •  Near Euljiro 1-ga Station, Line 2

One of the few Korean BBQ restaurants in Myeongdong that centres on seafood rather than pork or beef. Live shellfish displayed at the entrance; you select and grill at the table over a gas flame.

What to grill:

  • 홍합 Honghap (mussels) — pop open quickly; sweet and briny
  • 가리비 Garibi (scallops) — grill with butter; rich and meaty
  • 바지락 Bajirak (littleneck clams) — small and sweet; open in seconds
  • 키조개 Kijogae (pen shells) — large and firm; slice and sear in the shell

Walk from Insadong (~20 min, ~1.5 km): Head south through Tapgol Park, cross Jongno-ro, continue south into Myeongdong — Myeongdong 9-gil is a short turn left off the main strip.

Return to hotel: Taxi from Myeongdong to Gangnam (~25–30 min, ~₩14,000–₩20,000 via Kakao T).
